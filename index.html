Research Interests

My work sits at the intersection of computational social science and cybersecurity, drawing on agent-based simulation, network analysis, and empirical modeling to reason about defense under uncertainty.

i.

Cyber Attack Modeling

Curating empirical adversary intelligence from MITRE ATT&CK groups and campaigns, and encoding it as an Attack Flow Intelligence Network whose nodes and edges capture how real-world attacks actually unfold. Backbone extraction on this network reveals the routes adversaries traverse most often, letting simulations sample diverse, evidence-grounded attack trajectories in place of a handful of hand-crafted scenarios.

ii.

Agent-Based Simulation

Building high-fidelity organizational simulations in which heterogeneous employees, defenders, and adversaries co-evolve in continuous time. Grounded in computational human factors, these models quantify how phishing training, patching cadence, and detection policies bend the trajectory of hybrid phishing, watering-hole, and social-engineering campaigns.

iii.

Defense Optimization

Treating defense as a resource-allocation problem against an adaptive, observing adversary. Designing budget-constrained interdiction heuristics that remain robust when attackers re-plan around the defense, moving beyond the illusion of optimality that a single static strategy provides.
